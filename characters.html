The Fraggles

Five friends who live in the caves and dance their cares away!

Gobo Fraggle

The Explorer & Leader

Gobo Fraggle, the brave explorer of Fraggle Rock

Physical Description

Gobo is an orange-furred Fraggle with a distinctive purple-tipped nose and a tuft of darker orange hair on top of his head. He typically wears a brown explorer's vest that reflects his adventurous nature, and he's often seen carrying his guitar or one of his Uncle Traveling Matt's postcards.

About Gobo

Gobo is the natural-born explorer and de facto leader of the Fraggle Five. Brave, adventurous, and endlessly curious, Gobo loves nothing more than exploring the uncharted territories of Fraggle Rock and reading the postcards his Uncle Traveling Matt sends from "Outer Space" (our world). He is the central protagonist of the series and often drives the main storyline of each episode.

As the nephew of the legendary explorer Uncle Traveling Matt, Gobo feels a deep responsibility to follow in his uncle's footsteps. He's often the voice of reason among his friends, though his confidence can sometimes border on stubbornness. Gobo is also a talented guitarist and frequently leads the group in songs and musical numbers.

Role in the Show

Gobo serves as the main protagonist and often initiates the adventures that drive each episode's plot. His regular trips to Doc's workshop to collect his uncle's postcards create the connection between Fraggle Rock and the human world, establishing one of the show's central themes about interconnectedness.

Personality Traits

  • Brave and adventurous - always first to explore danger
  • Natural leader - others look to him for guidance
  • Endlessly curious - needs to know what lies beyond
  • Talented musician - plays guitar beautifully
  • Sometimes stubborn - can be overconfident

Fun Facts About Gobo

  • Gobo was performed by legendary Muppeteer Jerry Nelson
  • His name comes from the word "gobo," a screen used in film lighting
  • Gobo is the only Fraggle who regularly ventures into Outer Space

Red Fraggle

The Athlete & Daredevil

Red Fraggle, the athletic champion of Fraggle Rock

Physical Description

Red is a vibrant red-orange Fraggle with distinctive yellow-orange pigtails tied with purple bands. She has an energetic, athletic build and is almost always in motion. Her expressive face and bouncy movements perfectly capture her competitive, enthusiastic personality.

About Red

Red is the most athletic and competitive Fraggle in the Rock. With boundless energy and a fierce determination to be the best at everything she does, Red excels at sports, swimming, and any physical challenge you can throw at her. She holds more athletic records than any other Fraggle in Fraggle Rock history.

Though she can be impulsive and hot-headed, Red has a big heart and is fiercely loyal to her friends. Her best friend is Mokey, despite their opposite personalities, and she shares a competitive but affectionate relationship with Gobo. Red often stars in some of the series' most exciting episodes.

Role in the Show

Red represents the show's themes of physical achievement and the balance between competition and friendship. Her character arc often involves learning that winning isn't everything and that her friends value her for who she is, not her achievements. She frequently leads the group in athletic musical numbers.

Personality Traits

  • Athletic champion - excels at every sport
  • Fiercely competitive - hates to lose
  • Energetic & impulsive - acts before thinking
  • Confident bordering on cocky
  • Deeply caring despite tough exterior

Fun Facts About Red

  • Red was performed by Karen Prell throughout the original series
  • She is the best swimmer in all of Fraggle Rock
  • Red's friendship with the gentle Mokey shows that opposites attract

Mokey Fraggle

The Artist & Dreamer

Mokey Fraggle, the artistic soul of Fraggle Rock

Physical Description

Mokey is a lavender-purple Fraggle with long, flowing mauve hair that often falls gracefully over her shoulders. She has a gentle, dreamy expression and moves with an almost ethereal grace. Her soft features and calm demeanor reflect her peaceful, artistic nature.

About Mokey

Mokey is the gentle, artistic soul of the group. She finds beauty in everything around her and expresses herself through painting, poetry, and meditation. Mokey is deeply spiritual and has a special connection with Marjory the Trash Heap, whom she often visits for wisdom in the vast caverns of Fraggle Rock.

Despite her peaceful nature, Mokey is incredibly strong-willed and will stand up for her beliefs, especially when it comes to protecting others or preserving the beauty of Fraggle Rock. She's best friends with Red, and their opposite personalities create a perfect balance that the show explores in many episodes.

Role in the Show

Mokey represents creativity, spirituality, and environmental consciousness. She often serves as the moral compass of the group and her storylines frequently deal with themes of art, nature, and finding inner peace. Her regular visits to Marjory provide opportunities for the show's deeper philosophical messages.

Personality Traits

  • Artistic & deeply creative - expresses through multiple arts
  • Gentle & peaceful - rarely raises her voice
  • Spiritually connected - seeks wisdom from nature
  • Strong convictions - stands firm for beliefs
  • Sometimes dreamy - can be lost in thought

Fun Facts About Mokey

  • She writes poetry and keeps a detailed journal of Fraggle Rock life
  • Mokey harvests radishes from the Gorgs' Garden for the community

Wembley Fraggle

The Indecisive & Loyal Friend

Wembley Fraggle, the loyal heart of the Fraggle Five

Physical Description

Wembley is a small, yellow-green Fraggle with wide, expressive eyes that often show his internal struggle when making decisions. He has a distinctive shock of lighter yellow hair and wears his signature banana-tree print shirt. His nervous energy is visible in his fidgety movements.

About Wembley

Wembley is the sweetest and most lovable Fraggle you'll ever meet. His defining characteristic is his chronic indecisiveness—he can see both sides of every argument and struggles to make even the simplest choices. But what he lacks in decisiveness, he makes up for in loyalty and heart. Learn more about how his character develops across the 96 episodes of the series.

As Gobo's best friend and roommate, Wembley is always there to support his friends, even when he's terrified. He works as a Fireman for the Fraggle Rock Volunteer Fire Department, where his siren-playing skills come in handy. His character represents the show's message that you don't have to be the bravest or strongest to be valuable.

Role in the Show

Wembley often serves as the emotional heart of the series. His episodes typically deal with themes of self-doubt, loyalty, and finding courage within yourself. His indecisiveness creates both comedic moments and genuine character growth as he learns to trust his instincts.

Personality Traits

Boober Fraggle

The Worrier & Chef

Boober Fraggle, the cautious chef and laundry enthusiast

Physical Description

Boober is a blue-green Fraggle who is almost always seen wearing his signature red cap pulled down low over his eyes, giving him a perpetually worried appearance. He has a rounder body type than the other Fraggles and moves cautiously, always looking out for potential dangers.

About Boober

Boober is the cautious, practical member of the Fraggle Five. While his friends are off having adventures in the vast caves of Fraggle Rock, Boober prefers the safety and comfort of his cave, where he can indulge in his two favorite activities: cooking and doing laundry. His cooking is legendary, and his radish recipes are featured in several episodes.

Though he's often pessimistic and worries about everything that could possibly go wrong, Boober has a heart of gold and genuinely cares about his friends' well-being. His cooking is legendary in Fraggle Rock, and his famous radish bars are a particular favorite. He also has a secret alter ego named Sidebottom who represents his suppressed fun-loving side.

Role in the Show

Boober provides comic relief through his pessimism but also represents important themes about anxiety, facing fears, and the value of caution. His episodes often show him overcoming his fears to help his friends, demonstrating that bravery comes in many forms. His character connects to the show's music through several memorable songs about worry and fear.

  • Excellent cook - best chef in Fraggle Rock

The Doozers

Tiny, industrious builders who work all day, every day!

About the Doozers

The Doozers are a species of tiny, green, industrious creatures who stand only about 6 inches tall. They wear construction helmets and work clothes, and they love to build more than anything in the world. Unlike the playful Fraggles, Doozers find joy in constant work and construction.

Doozers spend their days constructing elaborate crystalline structures made from radishes harvested from the Gorgs' Garden. The amazing part? These beautiful constructions are meant to be eaten by the Fraggles! If the Fraggles didn't eat the Doozer structures, the Doozers would have nowhere left to build. This symbiotic relationship is central to the ecosystem of Fraggle Rock.

The contrast between the hard-working Doozers and the fun-loving Fraggles represents one of Jim Henson's key themes: different cultures can coexist and even depend on each other, even when their values seem completely opposite.

What Do Fraggles and Doozers Eat?

While Fraggles eat the delicious Doozer constructions (which taste like radishes), Doozers themselves eat a different diet consisting of various cave plants and processed radish materials. This creates a perfect ecosystem where nothing goes to waste.

Doozer Facts

  • Stand only 6 inches tall (compared to 18-inch Fraggles)
  • Build structures from processed radish dust called "Doozer sticks"
  • Work 24/7 and genuinely love it - work is their play
  • Have a highly organized society with architects, engineers, and builders
  • NEED Fraggles to eat their work - or they'd have nowhere to build

Doozers hard at work on their latest construction project

Notable Doozer Characters

Cotterpin Doozer

The Architect & Fraggle Ambassador

Cotterpin is a young, ambitious Doozer architect who breaks tradition by befriending Gobo Fraggle. She's the first Doozer to see Fraggles as intelligent beings rather than just mindless consumers of Doozer constructions. Her curiosity and open-mindedness make her a bridge between the two cultures.

Family: Daughter of Flange Doozer (father) and Wingnut Doozer (mother).

Notable Episodes: "All Work and All Play," "The Doozer Contest"

Fun Fact: Cotterpin was performed by Kathryn Mullen, who also performs Mokey Fraggle.

The Architect Doozer

Chief Designer & Leader

Cotterpin's mentor and one of the most respected leaders of Doozer society. The Architect is dedicated to grand building projects and maintaining Doozer traditions, though he eventually comes to appreciate Cotterpin's unconventional thinking.

Role: Represents traditional Doozer values and the importance of craftsmanship.

Wrench Doozer

Engineer & Mechanic

A skilled Doozer engineer who maintains the complex machinery used to process radishes into building materials. Wrench represents the technical expertise within Doozer society and the importance of keeping their construction equipment running smoothly.

Specialty: Maintains Doozer construction vehicles and mixing equipment.

Doozer Society

The Collective Community

Beyond individual characters, the Doozers represent a highly organized collective society. They work in teams, have specialized roles, and demonstrate remarkable engineering capabilities. Their society explores themes of industriousness, community, and finding purpose through work.

Population: Hundreds of Doozers live throughout Fraggle Rock's caves.

The Gorgs

The "royal family" of giants who tend the garden above Fraggle Rock

About the Gorgs

The Gorgs are a family of giant humanoid creatures who live in a castle in a garden adjacent to Fraggle Rock. They believe themselves to be the King and Queen of the Universe and their son to be the Crown Prince—though in reality, they may be the last of their species.

The Gorgs maintain an enormous garden where they grow radishes, which the Fraggles "steal" and the Doozers use for building. Junior Gorg spends much of his time trying (and failing) to catch the "Fwaggle" pests who raid the garden. This creates tension but also some of the series' funniest episodes.

Despite appearing as antagonists, the Gorgs are portrayed sympathetically. They're not truly evil—they simply see the world from a different perspective. The show's message about understanding different viewpoints is beautifully illustrated through the Fraggles' evolving relationship with these giants.

Are the Gorgs Evil?

No! While they may seem threatening to the tiny Fraggles, the Gorgs are portrayed as a loving family with their own concerns and traditions. Junior Gorg, in particular, develops genuine affection for the Fraggles over the course of the series.

The Gorg Royal Family in their garden kingdom

The Gorg Royal Family

Junior Gorg

Crown Prince of the Universe

The gentle giant son who befriends the Fraggles (especially Red) and learns that kindness is more important than catching "pests." Junior is tasked with guarding the radish garden but secretly admires the Fraggles' carefree lifestyle.

Character Arc: Junior transforms from a Fraggle-chasing menace to a gentle protector who questions his family's traditions.

Personality: Despite his enormous size, Junior is sweet-natured, somewhat naive, and desperately wants his parents' approval.

"I'm gonna get you, Fwaggles!"

Pa Gorg

King of the Universe (Self-Proclaimed)

The self-proclaimed King who rules with bluster but has a kind heart beneath his gruff exterior. Deeply devoted to his wife and son, Pa Gorg maintains the family's belief in their royal status despite having no other subjects.

Personality: Bombastic and proud, but ultimately loving and protective of his family.

Relationship: Adores Ma Gorg and is endlessly proud of Junior.

"I am the King of the Universe!"

Ma Gorg

Queen of the Universe

The practical and resourceful Queen who often has to manage both her husband and son. Loves her radish garden dearly and takes great pride in her cooking. Ma Gorg is the stabilizing force in the Gorg household.

Personality: Practical, nurturing, and the voice of reason in the Gorg family.

Specialties: Expert gardener and cook, famous for her radish recipes.

"Junior! Have you caught those Fwaggles yet?"

Outer Space (Our World)

The mysterious realm beyond Fraggle Rock—also known as our world!

About "Outer Space"

"Outer Space" is what Fraggles call the world outside their underground home—what we know as the human world. For Fraggles, venturing into Outer Space is both terrifying and fascinating. The connection between these worlds is maintained through a hole in the wall of Doc's workshop, which leads directly into the heart of Fraggle Rock.

This intersection of worlds represents one of the show's most brilliant storytelling devices. While the Fraggles learn about human culture through Uncle Traveling Matt's hilariously misinterpreted postcards, viewers see how two completely different perspectives can observe the same world.

Fun Fact: Different international versions of the show featured different human characters! The US had Doc and Sprocket, while the UK had The Captain and Sprocket on a lighthouse, and Germany had Doc as an inventor in his workshop.

The mysterious portal between two worlds

Human World Characters

Doc

The Inventor & Workshop Owner

Doc is the kind, curious human inventor who lives and works in the workshop connected to Fraggle Rock through a small hole in the wall. Completely unaware of the Fraggle civilization just beyond his workspace, Doc represents the human perspective on the show's themes of interconnection.

Full Name: Jerome "Doc" Crystal (revealed late in the series)

Personality: Kind-hearted, absent-minded, endlessly inventive. Doc talks to Sprocket constantly, treating him as both pet and confidant. He's patient and good-natured, often working on creative projects that hilariously parallel events in Fraggle Rock.

Role in the Show: Doc's scenes bookend each episode, providing the "real world" frame for Fraggle adventures. His storylines often thematically mirror the Fraggles' adventures without him knowing.

Character Arc: In the series finale, Doc finally learns the truth about Fraggles when he's about to move away—a beautiful culmination of the show's themes about connection and understanding.

Performer: Gerry Parkes portrayed Doc throughout the entire original series run from 1983-1987.

Sprocket

Doc's Loyal Dog & Fraggle Detector

Sprocket is Doc's faithful dog who serves as the comic bridge between the human and Fraggle worlds. Unlike Doc, Sprocket KNOWS the Fraggles exist—he frequently catches glimpses of Gobo when he comes through the hole to retrieve Uncle Matt's postcards!

The Eternal Frustration: Despite Sprocket's frantic attempts to alert Doc to the Fraggles' existence, Doc never believes him. This running gag creates both comedy and dramatic irony throughout the series.

Personality: Loyal, expressive, intelligent, and extremely frustrated by Doc's obliviousness. Sprocket communicates entirely through barks, whines, and physical comedy, yet audiences always understand exactly what he's trying to say.

Puppet Design: Sprocket was performed by Steve Whitmire, who later became the performer for Kermit the Frog. The puppet required incredible skill to convey complex emotions without words.

Relationship with Fraggles: While Sprocket initially sees the Fraggles as intruders, a mutual respect develops over time, especially with Gobo.

Uncle Traveling Matt

The Greatest Fraggle Explorer

Gobo's beloved uncle and the most famous explorer in all of Fraggle Rock! Uncle Traveling Matt ventured into "Outer Space" (our world) to study the mysterious "Silly Creatures" (humans) and sends regular postcards back to Gobo documenting his adventures and discoveries.

Exploration Mission: Matt believes he's conducting serious anthropological research on human society, but his complete misunderstanding of human culture creates the show's most hilarious moments.

Examples of His "Discoveries":

  • Thinks parking meters are hungry animals that humans feed coins to
  • Believes traffic lights are magical signal-givers that control Silly Creatures
  • Interprets department store mannequins as frozen Silly Creatures

Significance: Matt's segments offer brilliant social commentary, allowing adult viewers to see everyday human behavior from an outsider's perspective—often revealing how strange our customs actually are!

Performer: Dave Goelz brought Uncle Traveling Matt to life with perfect comic timing and sincerity.

Legacy: Uncle Matt's character influenced Gobo's own adventurous spirit and his dream of one day exploring Outer Space himself.

Supporting Characters

Other beloved inhabitants of Fraggle Rock

Marjory the Trash Heap

The all-knowing oracle—a sentient compost heap who dispenses wisdom to the Fraggles. Attended by her heralds Philo and Gunge. Her catchphrase "The Trash Heap has spoken! Nyeah!" became iconic among fans.

Cantus the Minstrel

A mysterious, philosophical Fraggle who wanders the Rock with his Magic Pipe. Performed by Jim Henson himself, Cantus represents wisdom and the spiritual power of music. Notable episodes: "The Minstrels," "The Honk of Honks."

The Storyteller

An elderly Fraggle who shares ancient tales and legends of Fraggle Rock's history. She preserves Fraggle culture through oral tradition.

The World's Oldest Fraggle

The ancient keeper of Fraggle Rock's rules and traditions, though he's often asleep. Despite his age, he holds crucial knowledge about Fraggle law.

Convincing John

A charismatic Fraggle with the power to convince anyone of anything through his songs. His persuasive abilities are legendary but sometimes misused.

Sidebottom

Boober's fun-loving, mischievous alter ego who emerges when Boober lets loose. Represents the suppressed playful side that cautious Boober keeps hidden.

Large Marvin

The largest Fraggle in the Rock, ironically gentle despite his intimidating size. Demonstrates that appearances can be deceiving and true strength comes from kindness.

Philo and Gunge

The two rat-like heralds who attend Marjory the Trash Heap. They announce visitors and occasionally offer their own (often unhelpful) advice alongside the oracle's wisdom.

Frequently Asked Questions About Fraggle Rock Characters

Common questions about the beloved inhabitants of Fraggle Rock

What are Fraggles supposed to be?

Fraggles are fictional creatures created by Jim Henson, designed to represent different aspects of human personality and culture. They are small, furry beings about 18 inches tall who live in an underground cave system. Each main Fraggle represents different human traits: Gobo embodies adventure and leadership, Red represents athleticism and competition, Mokey symbolizes creativity and spirituality, Wembley shows loyalty and indecisiveness, and Boober demonstrates caution and practicality. Henson designed them to show that diverse personalities can coexist harmoniously.

How many Fraggle Rock characters are there?

Fraggle Rock features over 50 named characters across its five-season run (1983-1987). The main cast includes 5 core Fraggles (Gobo, Red, Mokey, Wembley, and Boober), 3 Gorgs (Pa, Ma, and Junior), 2 human-world characters (Doc and Sprocket), Uncle Traveling Matt, and numerous supporting characters including Marjory the Trash Heap, Convincing John, Cantus the Minstrel, Cotterpin Doozer, The Storyteller, and the World's Oldest Fraggle.

Why is Wembley called Wembley in Fraggle Rock?

Wembley's name comes from the British slang term "wemble" or "wembling," which means to be indecisive or to waffle between choices. This perfectly captures his character—Wembley has extreme difficulty making decisions, often seeing both sides of every argument. His name is essentially a personality description, as "wembling" describes exactly what he does throughout the series. His catchphrase "I can't decide!" became a defining characteristic.

Who performed Gobo Fraggle?

Gobo Fraggle was performed by Jerry Nelson, a legendary Muppet performer who worked with Jim Henson for decades. Nelson also provided Gobo's singing voice. Other main Fraggle performers include Karen Prell (Red), Kathryn Mullen (Mokey), Steve Whitmire (Wembley), and Dave Goelz (Boober). Jim Henson himself performed Cantus the Minstrel, making those episodes particularly special to fans.

What do the Doozers build in Fraggle Rock?

Doozers build elaborate crystalline structures, towers, bridges, and architectural marvels using "Doozer sticks" made from processed radish dust. The fascinating part of their construction is that these buildings are designed to be eaten by Fraggles! This creates a perfect ecosystem—Doozers need Fraggles to eat their work so they have room to build more. If Fraggles stopped eating Doozer constructions, Doozers would eventually have nowhere left to build and become miserable.

Are the Gorgs villains in Fraggle Rock?

The Gorgs are not true villains—they're more misunderstood. Pa, Ma, and Junior Gorg see themselves as the royal family of the universe and view Fraggles as "pests" stealing their radishes. However, as the series progresses, especially Junior Gorg develops a more nuanced relationship with the Fraggles. By the series finale, the different species learn to coexist peacefully. The Gorgs represent how perspective shapes relationships—they're not evil, just operating from a different understanding.

Who is Marjory the Trash Heap?

Marjory the Trash Heap is a sentient, all-knowing compost pile who serves as the oracle and wisdom figure of Fraggle Rock. She dispenses advice (often cryptic) to Fraggles who seek her guidance, assisted by her heralds Philo and Gunge. Her catchphrase is "The Trash Heap has spoken! Nyeah!" Marjory represents the wisdom of the natural world and shows that knowledge can come from unexpected sources. She was performed by Jerry Nelson.

What happened to Uncle Traveling Matt?

Uncle Traveling Matt left Fraggle Rock in the very first episode to explore "Outer Space" (the human world). Throughout the series, he sends postcards back to his nephew Gobo describing his adventures and observations about "Silly Creatures" (humans). He frequently misunderstands human technology and customs, leading to humorous commentary. Uncle Matt returns to Fraggle Rock in the series finale "Change of Address" for a heartfelt reunion with Gobo and the other Fraggles.

Who is the leader of the Fraggles?

Fraggle society doesn't have a formal leader—it's an egalitarian community where everyone is equal. However, Gobo Fraggle often emerges as the natural leader of the main five Fraggles due to his adventurous spirit, confidence, and ability to make decisions. When the group faces challenges, the others typically look to Gobo for direction. For matters of tradition and rules, the World's Oldest Fraggle serves as the authority figure, while Marjory the Trash Heap acts as the community's spiritual advisor and oracle.

What is the difference between Fraggles and Doozers?

Fraggles and Doozers are two completely different species with opposite philosophies. Fraggles are 18-inch-tall furry creatures who love to play, sing, swim, and explore—their motto could be "work is for fools." Doozers are tiny 6-inch-tall green creatures wearing hard hats who live to work and build. Doozers construct elaborate structures from processed radishes, while Fraggles eat these constructions (which actually helps the Doozers by giving them space to build more). This symbiotic relationship shows how different cultures can depend on each other despite having completely different values.

Are there human characters in Fraggle Rock?

Yes! The main human character is Doc (full name Jerome "Doc" Crystal), an inventor who lives in a workshop connected to Fraggle Rock through a hole in the wall. His loyal dog Sprocket also plays a major role—Sprocket knows the Fraggles exist but can never convince Doc they're real. Different international versions featured different human characters: the US had Doc in his workshop, the UK had The Captain in a lighthouse, and Germany had Doc as an inventor. These human scenes bookend each episode and thematically connect to the Fraggles' adventures.

Which Fraggle is the bravest?

This depends on how you define bravery! Gobo Fraggle is traditionally considered the bravest because he regularly ventures to "Outer Space" (the human world) to retrieve Uncle Matt's postcards, facing the terrifying Sprocket. However, Red Fraggle shows incredible physical courage through her athletic feats and competitive nature. Interestingly, some argue the most courageous is actually Boober—despite being terrified of almost everything, he still faces his fears when his friends need him. True bravery might be acting despite fear, which makes Boober's moments of courage especially meaningful.

What do Fraggles eat?

Fraggles have a varied diet that includes radishes (their primary food source, stolen from the Gorgs' garden), Doozer constructions (crystalline structures made from processed radishes that taste delicious to Fraggles), various cave mushrooms and plants, and fruits. Radishes are particularly important to Fraggle culture—they're used for food, celebrations, and even art. Mokey Fraggle has the dangerous job of harvesting radishes from the Gorgs' garden, making her the community's primary food gatherer. Eating Doozer constructions is actually essential to the Rock's ecosystem, as it gives Doozers space to build more.
