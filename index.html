Jim Henson's Vision of World Peace

"My goal is to bring about world peace" - Jim Henson

Fraggle Rock wasn't just a children's show—it was a revolutionary experiment in international cooperation and cultural understanding.

Premiering on HBO on January 10, 1983, Fraggle Rock became the first truly international co-production, designed from its inception to resonate with global audiences. The show ran for 5 glorious seasons until 1987, featuring 96 unforgettable episodes and over 190 original songs.

Jim Henson created this magical world to teach children and adults alike about the interconnectedness of all living things—how our actions affect others, and how understanding different perspectives leads to harmony.

Historic First: Fraggle Rock was the first North American TV show broadcast in the Soviet Union in 1989!

The Interconnected World

Four worlds, one ecosystem—discover how everything is connected

Fraggle Rock

A vast network of caves where the Fraggles live, work only 30 minutes per week, and spend their time playing, singing, and exploring.

Doozer Construction

Industrious tiny creatures who love to build beautiful crystalline structures from radishes—which the Fraggles happily eat!

Gorg's Garden

The giant Gorg family (Ma, Pa, and Junior) maintain a magnificent garden that grows the radishes everyone depends on.

Outer Space

Our world! Connected to Fraggle Rock through a hole in Doc's workshop wall, where his dog Sprocket occasionally spots Fraggles.

The Heart of Fraggle Rock

More than entertainment—lessons for life

Environmental Interconnectedness

Every creature depends on others in the ecosystem—Fraggles eat Doozer constructions, Doozers use Gorg radishes, and the cycle continues.

Cultural Understanding

Different species with different perspectives learn to coexist peacefully through communication and empathy.

Peace & Anti-War

Conflicts are resolved through understanding, not violence. The show promotes peaceful coexistence and cooperation.

Work-Life Balance

Fraggles work only 30 minutes per week, celebrating play, creativity, and joy as equally important to productivity.
